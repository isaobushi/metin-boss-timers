DungeonAid — Privacy Policy

Effective 8 June 2026

DungeonAid is a read-only timer overlay for tracking boss spawns and dungeon cooldowns. This policy covers both the desktop application (distributed via the Microsoft Store) and the in-browser web demo at metin-boss-timers.vercel.app.

The short version

DungeonAid collects no data about you. It has no accounts, no analytics, no telemetry, no advertising, and no third-party trackers. The application makes no network calls of any kind.

Information we collect

None. We do not collect, store, transmit, or sell any personal information, usage data, or device identifiers.

Data stored on your device

Your configuration — boss list, timer settings, and the cooldown catalog — is saved locally on your own device (on the desktop app, in its local application data; in the web demo, in your browser's localStorage). This data never leaves your device and is never sent to us or anyone else. You can clear it at any time by resetting the app or clearing your browser storage.

Network and third parties

The desktop application does not connect to the internet, so no data is shared with any third party. The web demo is served as static files and likewise makes no data-collecting requests. We do not use any third-party analytics or advertising SDKs.

Children's privacy

Because DungeonAid collects no personal information from anyone, it collects none from children.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a revised effective date.

Contact

Questions about this policy can be raised via the project's public repository: github.com/isaobushi/metin-boss-timers/issues.

DungeonAid is an independent, unofficial tool. It works with Metin2 but is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or licensed by Gameforge.
